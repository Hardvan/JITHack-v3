Model Description

The model was trained on a dataset containing various health-related features, aiming to predict the likelihood of a heart attack. The dataset included information such as age, sex, exercise-induced angina, chest pain type, resting blood pressure, cholesterol levels, fasting blood sugar, and other relevant factors. Three different models were implemented for comparison: Naive Bayes, Random Forest, and a Deep Learning model using Artificial Neural Networks (ANN). The dataset was preprocessed by removing non-useful columns and exploring the data through visualizations and statistical analysis. The Naive Bayes and Random Forest models demonstrated decent performance, but the Deep Learning model, implemented as an ANN, outperformed them in terms of accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score. The ANN architecture included two hidden layers with 100 and 50 neurons, respectively, and a final sigmoid activation layer for binary classification. The model was trained on scaled data and exhibited robust performance during evaluation. In conclusion, the Neural Network model proved to be the most effective for predicting the likelihood of a heart attack in this dataset, showcasing the superiority of deep learning techniques in complex tasks. The model was saved for future use, providing a powerful tool for heart attack prediction in clinical applications.
